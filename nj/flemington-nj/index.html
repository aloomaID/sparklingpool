How you can prevent algae from growing in my pool?

Preventing algae from growing in your pool is essential for maintaining a clean and safe swimming environment. At Spark Pool Cleaning, we understand the challenges of keeping your pool algae-free, especially in Flemington New Jersey where warm weather can promote rapid growth. Here are some effective strategies to help you prevent algae blooms in your pool.
First, ensure that your pool’s chemical levels are balanced. Regularly test the pH, alkalinity, and chlorine levels to keep them within the recommended ranges. Proper chemical balance not only sanitizes your pool but also creates an environment that is less hospitable to algae.
Second, maintain consistent circulation and filtration. Running your pool pump for at least 8-12 hours a day helps keep water moving, preventing stagnant areas where algae can thrive. Regularly clean or replace your pool filters to ensure they are functioning optimally.
Third, keep your pool clean by regularly brushing and vacuuming the surfaces. Remove debris, leaves, and organic matter that can contribute to algae growth. Additionally, consider using an algaecide as a preventive measure, especially during warmer months.
Lastly, cover your pool when not in use to minimize debris accumulation and block sunlight, which encourages algae growth. By implementing these simple yet effective strategies, you can enjoy a beautiful, algae-free pool all season long. Flemington, NJ Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Flemington is a borough in and the county seat of Hunterdon County, in the U.S. state of New Jersey. Most of the borough is located in the Amwell Valley, a low-lying area of the Newark Basin, and the Raritan Valley, the South Branch of the Raritan River, which flows through the center of Flemington. Northwestern portions of the borough sit on the Hunterdon Plateau. As of the 2020 United States census, the borough's population was 4,876, its highest decennial count ever and an increase of 295 (+6.4%) from the 2010 census count of 4,581, which in turn reflected an increase of 381 (+9.1%) from the 4,200 counted in the 2000 census.
